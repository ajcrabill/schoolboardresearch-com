Literature Review

What Does "Effective Board Governance" Actually Predict? A Review of the Literature

Key Finding: Research traditions differ in how they define "effective governance," but converge on outcome monitoring as the governance practice most consistently associated with improved student results.
Methodology: Synthesis of multiple peer-reviewed studies and RAND policy reports; correlational and quasi-experimental designs.
For Practitioners: Boards should distinguish between process fidelity (doing governance "correctly") and outcome accountability (monitoring whether results actually improve) — research supports the latter more strongly.

How We Score Research

We evaluate each study using the PEARLS framework — a 6-criterion, 30-point scale that scores participant quality, execution rigor, analysis precision, relevance, funding transparency, and scope. Below 20 = untrustworthy for decision-making. Above 26 = trustworthy.

How to Use the Findings

Practitioner implications are clearly labeled and separated from research findings. Research informs — it does not prescribe. Context, local conditions, and professional judgment remain essential.
